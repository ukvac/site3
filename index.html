The current problem

Education records are scattered across a lifetime of institutions.

A single citizen's history — school, A-Levels, university, vocational training, employer certificates — sits in filing cabinets and separate databases across schools, universities, training providers, examination authorities and employers. Nobody holds the whole picture.

FOR CITIZENS

Lost paperwork, repeated proof

Certificates get damaged or lost, and the same documents are re-submitted for every job or course application.

FOR EMPLOYERS

Slow, manual verification

Checking qualifications by hand is slow and leaves room for fraudulent certificates to slip through.

FOR GOVERNMENT

No national skills picture

Without unified data, planning education funding and workforce policy relies on guesswork.

The proposed solution

A secure digital profile that follows every citizen through school, training and work.

Four connected portals share one verified record — each institution confirms what it taught, each citizen controls what they share, and government sees the aggregate picture without touching personal data.

Citizen control

The citizen decides who sees what, and for how long.

Every share is scoped and reversible: a specific set of records, a specific viewer, a specific expiry date. Nothing is visible by default.

Rollout

A three-phase implementation path.

PHASE 1

Minimum viable platform

Citizen profiles, certificate uploads, digital portfolio, institution verification, employer sharing.

PHASE 2

Education integration

Direct connections to schools, universities, training centres and examination bodies for automatic issuing.

PHASE 3

National expansion

AI career guidance, skills analytics, government dashboards and workforce intelligence tools.

Long-term vision

From static paper certificates to a living digital identity.

A platform where every citizen owns a trusted record of their education journey, every employer can confidently identify talent, and government can plan with real skills data — the foundation of a national digital skills economy.

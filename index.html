Fee Explanation Scenarios

This page shows example fee structures and possible explanations for why a user may be asked to pay a revised balance.

Fee Structures

Scenario A

Outstanding Balance Review

£529
  • Partial waiver applied rather than full waiver.
  • Case reviewed and a remaining balance became payable.
  • May reflect reassessment of financial eligibility.
  • Could be presented as an outstanding balance before progression.
Example explanation: “Following review, a remaining balance of £529 is payable before the application can proceed.”

Scenario B

Route Adjustment Fee

£479
  • Application moved to a different internal fee category.
  • Original route selection may have been amended on review.
  • System recalculation triggered a revised balance.
  • Used in training to simulate administrative recategorisation.
Example explanation: “Your application has been reassessed under a revised category and an updated fee of £479 is now due.”
View explanation Case reference
Scenario C

Document Revalidation Charge

£349
  • Used as a mock example for a revalidation or reprocessing stage.
  • Can simulate missing-document follow-up costs.
  • May represent a reduced balance after initial review.
  • Useful in awareness training around unexpected fee requests.
Example explanation: “Additional document review has resulted in a revised processing charge of £349.”
View explanation Case reference
Scenario D

Linked Applicant Balance

£189
  • Main applicant covered, linked applicant not fully covered.
  • Represents a smaller residual fee in a multi-person case.
  • Good for testing whether users spot applicant-specific wording.
  • Helps simulate confusion around who is covered by a waiver.
Example explanation: “The approved waiver applies only to one part of the case. A balance of £189 remains for a linked applicant.”

Scenario E

Enhanced Review Service

£649
  • Training-only example of an upgraded review or handling tier.
  • Can be used to test reactions to time-pressure language.
  • Useful in phishing-awareness simulations.
  • Shows how vague “service upgrade” wording can appear plausible.
Example explanation: “Your case has been placed into an enhanced review queue and a revised fee of £649 has been applied.”
